Currently I am exploring changes in gene expression during aging with the labs of Martin Hetzer and Saket Navlakha In particular I am interested in understanding if there are molecular markers that can predict "biological age" as distinct from chronological age. A method to correctly predict biological age would be useful in understanding the aging process, and would have applications in the evaluation of treatments and in the clinic. In collaboration with the labs of Satchidananda Panda and Horacio de la Iglesia, I am analyzing human health data from clinical measures, actiwatches, and smartphone apps. This combination of data allows us to investigate relationships between circadian rhythms, sleep patterns, light exposure, behavior, and glucose regulation. A better understanding of the interplay between circadian and metabolic processes will help us develop recommendations for a healthy lifestyle and good public policy. In previous work I used computational neural models to ask questions such as: How are memories formed and maintained? How are those memories used to generate flexible behavior as opposed to more rigid conditioned responses? How do the complex interactions between brain, body, and environment generate adaptive responses? ## Blog posts
